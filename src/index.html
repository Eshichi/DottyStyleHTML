Background
& Audience
Tourism & Events Queensland wanted to increase consideration
of The Whitsundays amongst 'social fun seekers'; digitally savvy
consumers that socialise substantially online.
Problem
The Whitsundays was losing its cachet as an aspirational
holiday location.
Objective
Shift perceptions and re-build the Whitsundays' social currency
as a world-class destination.
Solution
We knew movies rated high on the list of our target's interest
and, with increadible beaches, seaplanes, yatch and glamorous
locations, the Whitsundays is the perfect setting for a movie.
So we invented Austrailians to write it:
The Whitsundays.
A movie that's waiting to be written.
We enlisted world-renowned screenwritter Craig Pearce as Script
Supervisor. Then for 20 days we posted a daily storyboard image
on Facebook and Instagram, inviiting the public to write scenes
for a chance to win $10,000 luxury holiday.
The campaign launched with a trailer, radio and online
advertising.
We reached to the storyline as it developed, selecting and
treating images according to the previous winning scene.
Each scene written and shared became a compelling "ad" for
the Whitsundays.
Together, a Hollywood screenwritter and the Austrailian public
had created the world's first crowd-sourced movie storyboard
and a story universe with millions of possible premutations.
